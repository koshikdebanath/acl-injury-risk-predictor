An Attention-Based Deep Learning Approach to Knee Injury Classification from MRI Images

K. Debanath, A.F.M.M. Rahman, M.A. Hossain
Cox's Bazar, Bangladesh
2023 26th International Conference on Computer and Information Technology (ICCIT)
Paper Code IEEE Xplore DOI

Our attention-based deep learning model identifies and prioritizes critical regions in knee MRI images to accurately classify various knee injuries including ACL tears, meniscal tears, and other abnormalities.

Abstract

Knee injuries are among the most common musculoskeletal conditions, with accurate diagnosis critical for effective treatment planning. Traditional diagnosis methods rely heavily on manual interpretation of MRI scans by radiologists, which can be time-consuming and subject to inter-observer variability. This paper presents an attention-based deep learning approach to automatically classify knee injuries from MRI images with improved accuracy and interpretability.

Our proposed model integrates attention mechanisms into a convolutional neural network (CNN) architecture, enabling the network to focus on the most relevant anatomical regions for injury classification. The attention module learns to weight different spatial locations in the MRI images, effectively highlighting areas such as the anterior cruciate ligament (ACL), menisci, and cartilage that are most informative for diagnosis. This not only improves classification performance but also provides visual explanations of the model's decision-making process.

We evaluate our approach on a comprehensive dataset of knee MRI scans, demonstrating superior performance compared to baseline CNN models without attention mechanisms. The attention-based model achieves high accuracy in classifying various knee injuries including ACL tears, meniscal tears, and cartilage abnormalities. Experimental results show that the integration of attention mechanisms leads to more robust and interpretable predictions, making the model more suitable for clinical deployment. The visualization of attention maps provides valuable insights for clinicians, potentially serving as a decision support tool in radiological practice.

Methodology

Attention Mechanism Architecture

Our proposed attention-based deep learning model integrates spatial attention mechanisms into a convolutional neural network backbone. The attention module learns to assign importance weights to different regions of the MRI image, allowing the network to focus on anatomically relevant features for injury classification.

Figure 1: Attention mechanism prioritizing critical regions in knee MRI for injury detection

Model Components

  • Backbone Network: Deep CNN architecture for feature extraction from MRI images
  • Attention Module: Spatial attention layer that generates importance weights for different image regions
  • Feature Fusion: Weighted combination of attention-modulated features
  • Classification Head: Fully connected layers for multi-class injury prediction

Datasets

We conducted experiments on two complementary datasets to evaluate our attention-based approach across different classification tasks: binary ACL tear detection and multi-class knee injury classification.

  • Image resizing to 224×224 pixels
  • Intensity normalization to [0, 1] range

Key Findings

  • Superior Performance: Our attention-based model achieved 93.4% accuracy on binary classification and 88.9% on multi-class tasks, outperforming all baseline models
  • Improved Generalization: Consistent performance across different injury types demonstrates robust feature learning
  • Interpretability: Attention maps provide visual explanations highlighting anatomically relevant regions
  • Efficiency: Competitive parameter count (14.2M) compared to larger architectures like VGG-16 (138.4M)
  • Clinical Relevance: High precision and recall metrics make the model suitable for clinical decision support
